What is @farawayplanets?

@farawayplanets is a TwitterBot that illustrates the vastness of space and the passage of time by tweeting whenever news of historical events makes it to known exoplanets (planets in other solar systems). Since light travels at a finite speed, distant observers will see Earth as it looked at some point in the past. How far into the past each observer can see is proportional to their distance. For example, if there were observers on a planet 10 light-years from Earth, they would see Earth as it appeared 10 years ago.

@farawayplanets calculates what date observers on known exoplanets would see if they could see Earthling calendars through their telescopes right now. It then scans through On this day in history-type Wikipedia pages like this one to find events that occurred on those dates. At the end of each tweet, the Earth date for the historical event is given in parentheses.

@farawayplanets is meant to be an educational novelty that demonstrates the huge distances between the known planets, the finite speed of light, and the passage of time.

How does it work?

Under the hood, @farawayplanets makes use of

  • Python
  • Tweepy for posting to Twitter
  • Some guts of OSCAAR for managing the exoplanets.org data
  • With a catalog of exoplanets from the great exoplanets.org
