Brett M. Morris

 Graduate Student of Astronomy and Astrobiology at the University of Washington

Quick Bio

I'm a second year graduate student in the dual-title PhD Astronomy and Astrobiology Program at the University of Washington in Seattle. I work with Eric Agol (UW) on characterizing exoplanet systems with transit timing variations with Kepler, and I work with Avi Mandell (NASA GSFC) on characterizing exoplanets with transmission spectroscopy in the near-infrared.

In 2013 I was a research associate at the Goddard Center for Astrobiology with Avi Mandell at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, MD. I used near-infrared spectroscopy to study extrasolar planet atmospheres with the MOSFIRE instrument at Keck Observatory and worked with Kepler data to study the super-hot transiting planet HAT-P-7 b. In February 2013 I published a Letter to the Astrophysical Journal on my work on Kepler's Optical Secondary Eclipse of HAT-P-7b and Probable Detection of Planet-Induced Stellar Gravity Darkening. Check it out!

In December 2012 I graduated with a B.S. in Astronomy with High Honors from the Department of Astronomy, and a B.S. in Physics from the University of Maryland, College Park. There I worked Derek Richardson on numerical simulations of contact binary asteroids and with Drake Deming on space and ground-based photometry of exoplanets.
