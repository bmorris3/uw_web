Welcome!

I'm currently a first year graduate student at the University of Washington, Seattle. I'm pursuing a PhD in astronomy and astrobiology.

Recent news: My open source photometry code project OSCAAR was recently featured in an awesome NASA press release!

From January to August of 2013 I was a research assistant at the Goddard Center for Astrobiology at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, MD. I used near-infrared spectroscopy to study extrasolar planet atmospheres with Keck. In February 2013 I published a Letter to the Astrophysical Journal on my work on Kepler's Optical Secondary Eclipse of HAT-P-7b and Probable Detection of Planet-Induced Stellar Gravity Darkening. Check it out!

In December 2012 I graduated with a B.S. in Astronomy with High Honors from the Department of Astronomy, and a B.S. in Physics from the University of Maryland, College Park. I'm originally from Long Island, NY. I'm interested in extrasolar planets, astrobiology and computational astrophysics.
