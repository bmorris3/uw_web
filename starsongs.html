Sonifying stars

Making the host stars of habitable worlds sing

Here we will experiment with turning the measurements of brightness over time into sound waves. This is a project by Brett Morris and Shin Yu Pai.

Planet properties sourced from the NASA Exoplanet Archive.

Kepler-296

Kepler-296 e and f are potentially habitable worlds, which are 1.5 and 1.8 Earth radii in size and orbit their star every 76 and 115 days, respectively.

TRAPPIST-1

TRAPPIST-1 f, g and h are potentially habitable worlds, which are 1.0, 1.1 and 0.75 Earth radii in size and orbit their star every 9, 12 and 18 days, respectively.

Kepler-1229

Kepler-1229 b is a potentially habitable world, which is 1.4 Earth radii in size and orbits its star once every 87 days.

Kepler-62

Kepler-62 e and f are potentially habitable worlds, which are 1.6 and 1.4 Earth radii in size and orbit their star every 122 and 268 days, respectively.

Kepler-442

Kepler-442 b is a potentially habitable world, which is 1.3 Earth radii in size and orbits its star every 112 days.

Kepler-186

Kepler-186 f is a potentially habitable world, which is 1.1 Earth radii in size and orbits its star every 130 days.
