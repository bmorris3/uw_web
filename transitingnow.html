What is @transitingnow?

@transitingnow is a TwitterBot that tweets in the middle of exoplanet transit events -- which occur when a planet in another solar system (an exoplanet) appears to pass in front of its star, causing some of the starlight to be blocked out from our perspective on Earth, allowing us to detect the planet's presence.

It accesses the catalog of exoplanets from exoplanets.org and uses the published mid-transit times and orbital periods measured during past transits to extrapolate to future times of transit. It also serves up a mix of facts about the planets like their radii, their orbital distances from their host stars, the temperatures of their host stars, the distances from Earth to the the exoplanet and the constellation that the stars appear in (when these data are available).

@transitingnow is meant to be an educational novelty that demonstrates the vast number of transiting planets discovered. Although no exoplanets were known when @transitingnow's maker was born, today there is a known exoplanet transit happening practically every minute of the day somewhere in the sky.

How does it work?

Under the hood, @transitingnow makes use of:

  • Python
  • Tweepy for posting to Twitter
  • Some guts of OSCAAR for managing the exoplanets.org data
  • astropy for converting between timing conventions
  • PyEphem for finding which constellation each planet is in
  • With a catalog of exoplanets from the great exoplanets.org
